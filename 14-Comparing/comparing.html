Comparing Elementary Sorting Algorithms

In the grand scheme, these three are pretty close to each other in most scenarios. For small data sets, they are pretty reasonable. Slow down a lot when the data gets larger. To get faster, we need far more complex algorithms.

Both Bubble and Insertion sort are good at nearly sorted data. Whereas Selection is very bad at that.

All three of these are great when it comes to Space Complexity. They take more time, but they don't write many variables or create new arrays.

Insertion is great if data is streaming in.
