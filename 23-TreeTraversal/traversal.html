Tree Traversal

Given any tree, even special ones, there are two main ways of tree traversal- Breadth First Search and Depth First Search.

Breadth First Search

Starting from the beginning, BFS will look through the tree by generation. All of the children from any parent at a certain level, then all of their children, then all of their children and so on. Essentially goes along the x-axis across the tree.

CTD. This is for Binary Tree, but doesn't have to be.

Depth First Search

Starting from the root node, DFS will look through down the y-axis first, visiting all of the nodes along the left side and then moving to the right for example.

Unlike BFS, there are several DFS algorithms. We have a couple of options on how we want to show our data.

The PreOrder algorithm will explore the entire left side before moving onto the right.
